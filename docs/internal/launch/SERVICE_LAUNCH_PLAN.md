# Service Launch Plan — Circle MCP Server

> **Version:** 1.0.0
> **Last Updated:** 2026-03-13
> **Purpose:** Operating document for early commercial launch of Circle MCP as a product + service business

---

## Launch Objective

Start generating revenue, collecting feedback, and building reputation within 30 days by selling guided setup services and distributing the infrastructure product to Circle operators, consultants, and agencies.

**Not** a mass-market launch. This is a founder-led, high-signal introduction to a specific professional audience.

---

## Who We Are Targeting First

### Tier 1 — Immediate Outreach (Week 1–2)

| Segment | Why First | Expected Response |
|---------|-----------|-------------------|
| **Circle consultants** | Already sell Circle services. This adds a new capability to their offering. Low education needed. | High — direct revenue opportunity for them |
| **Community agencies** | Manage multiple Circle communities. Force-multiplier use case. | High — operational efficiency is their business |

### Tier 2 — Fast Follow (Week 2–3)

| Segment | Why Second | Expected Response |
|---------|------------|-------------------|
| **Advanced community operators** | Power users who want AI leverage. Need less hand-holding. | Medium-High — they will self-qualify |

### Tier 3 — Expand (Week 3–4)

| Segment | Why Third | Expected Response |
|---------|-----------|-------------------|
| **Educators / cohort builders** | Clear use case but may need more guidance on setup. | Medium — strong once they see the workflow |
| **Creator businesses** | Community-led brands using Circle. Value content automation. | Medium-Low — need proof of value first |

## What We Are Selling First

### Primary: Guided Setup Service

> "We set up Circle MCP for your community, train your team, and hand over a working AI community operations layer."

This is the revenue engine for v1. It converts immediately because the product requires guided onboarding at this maturity stage.

### Secondary: Open-Source Distribution

The GitHub repo and hosted endpoint build credibility and create inbound interest. Technical users who discover the repo may convert to service clients, or become advocates.

### Tertiary: Smithery Marketplace Listing

Once published, Smithery provides passive discovery within the MCP ecosystem. Low-effort, low-maintenance channel.

## Founder Priorities

### Do This

1. **Sell to consultants and agencies first.** They understand the value immediately and have budget.
2. **Deliver exceptional first engagements.** First 3 clients set the quality standard and generate referrals.
3. **Collect feedback aggressively.** Every session should end with "what would make this more useful?"
4. **Post proof publicly.** LinkedIn posts with real results beat any marketing copy.
5. **Keep the product honest.** Ship what works. Document what does not.

### Do Not Do This

1. **Do not build a marketing website yet.** GitHub README + LinkedIn + direct outreach is enough for v1.
2. **Do not automate outreach.** Personal, targeted messages to 30–50 people beat mass campaigns.
3. **Do not discount aggressively.** The service is premium because the value is real. Discounting signals uncertainty.
4. **Do not chase low-intent users.** If someone needs hand-holding on what MCP is, they are not your v1 buyer.
5. **Do not build new features before selling.** The 16 tools are enough. Sell first, iterate based on demand.
6. **Do not create elaborate funnels or landing pages.** A LinkedIn profile, GitHub repo, and direct messages are the v1 GTM stack.

## How Product and Services Reinforce Each Other

```
Product Improvements                    Service Revenue
───────────────────                     ───────────────
Better docs → easier setup            → lower delivery cost per client
More tools → broader use cases        → higher willingness to pay
Hosted endpoint → no local install    → unlocks non-technical buyers
Smithery listing → passive discovery  → inbound service leads
Client feedback → product roadmap     → features that sell
Testimonials → credibility            → higher conversion rate
```

The product is the infrastructure. The service is the distribution engine. Neither works as well alone.

**v1 revenue comes from services.** Services fund product development. Product improvements reduce service delivery cost and attract self-serve users (v2/v3).

## What Not To Do Yet

| Temptation | Why Not Yet |
|------------|-------------|
| Build a landing page / marketing site | Premature. GitHub README + LinkedIn is sufficient for 30 outreach targets. |
| Pursue partnerships with Circle.so | Too early. Need 5+ paying clients and a track record first. |
| Create a free tier / freemium model | The product is already open source. Services are the revenue layer. No need to create another free thing. |
| Hire a marketer or salesperson | Founder-led sales is the right motion for the first 10 clients. |
| Build a dashboard or analytics UI | The CLI/MCP interface is sufficient for v1 buyers. Dashboard is a v3 feature. |
| Expand to other platforms | Circle-first. Depth beats breadth at this stage. |

## Risk Register

| Risk | Probability | Impact | Mitigation |
|------|-------------|--------|------------|
| Low outreach response rate | Medium | Medium | A/B test messaging, target warmer contacts first, lead with demo/proof |
| Circle API changes break tools | Low | High | Pin to v2 API, monitor Circle changelog, maintain test suite |
| Token rotation not completed | High | High | **Must rotate before any public launch activity** |
| First client has bad experience | Low | High | Over-prepare, run internal dry-run before first paid session |
| Competitor enters market | Low | Medium | First-mover advantage + depth of tooling + service relationship |
